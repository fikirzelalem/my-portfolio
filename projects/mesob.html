Mesob

A recipe platform and blog celebrating Ethiopian and African cuisine, built with a clean, content-first design.

About the Project

Mesob is a recipe platform and food blog dedicated to Ethiopian and broader African cuisine. The name comes from the traditional woven basket table used in Ethiopian culture to serve injera and communal dishes, a fitting symbol for a project about bringing people together through food.

The site features curated recipes with step-by-step instructions, cultural context for dishes, and a blog section covering food history and cooking tips. The design prioritizes the content: clean typography, generous white space, and warm color tones that reflect the richness of the cuisine.

This was a personal passion project combining two things I care about: building on the web and celebrating my culture. It's fully live and accessible to anyone who wants to explore Ethiopian and African cooking.

Key Features

Recipe Library

Curated collection of Ethiopian and African recipes with step-by-step instructions, ingredient lists, and serving suggestions.

Cultural Context

Each dish includes background on its cultural significance, regional variations, and the traditions tied to how it's prepared and shared.

Food Blog

Written articles covering Ethiopian food history, cooking techniques, and the stories behind iconic dishes like injera and doro wat.

Responsive Design

Clean, content-first layout built to look great on any screen size, from mobile browsing in the kitchen to desktop reading.
